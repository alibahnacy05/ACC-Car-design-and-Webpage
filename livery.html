Livery Design Of

Porsche 911 GT3 R

First steps


The first thing I did was look for a car template that I wanted to design and becuase of my huge love for Porsche, I decided to settle on the Porsche 911 GT3 R.


At first, looking at the template on photoshop left me confused and overwhelmed but then I took a closer look and realized that some car parts are in order and most are scattered around on the template


Now the challenge here was to make sure that the car parts I was coloring were not going to be on the wrong part of the car and mess my design up.

Step by Step


I found out you can make multiple selections on the same layer by using the polygonal lasso tool, the next step/challenge from here was to make sure that I designing the exact parts I wanted to.


The solution to this was to make different selections aligned with the UV layer in the template, fill it in with a base color and add a gradient overlay to achieve a blend/fade effect on the colors. I did this with red fading into yellow and the blue fading into green.


After finishing the main and obvious/easy parts of the car, it was time to move on to the harder and smaller parts that were scattered around. I found it easier to make multiple layers for each one and be precise with my colors to get the most appealing look. I did this by a trial and error method, checking the design continuously on acc-liveries.vercel.app.

The final steps


After finally finishing the coloring of my car, it was now time to add the png's of my sponsor Google onto the car. I decided to add the chrome logo that we all know to the roof of my car and some of the Google apps such as G-mail and Google Scholar to the back left and right side of the car.


In addition, I added "Google+" on the spoiler of the car to make sure it is obvious who is sponsoring plus a few flames at the front of the car to give it a firey and fast look.


Overall, my car is completely inspired and sponsored by Google, from the colors of the car to the stickers and addtion of the "G = Google" sticker on the hood.
